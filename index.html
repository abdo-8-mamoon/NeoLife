Who We Are

Pioneering Neonatal
Care Since 1998

NeoLife NICU is a Level IV Neonatal Intensive Care Unit — the highest designation — offering subspecialty care for the most complex neonatal conditions.

Ranked #1 in Neonatal Care Joint Commission Gold Seal of Approval — 10 consecutive years

Level IV NICU Designation

The highest level of neonatal care available, equipped for the most complex conditions including congenital heart defects, respiratory distress syndrome, and extremely premature births.

Family-Centered Care Model

We believe families are essential partners in care. Our open visitation policy, family rooms, and dedicated parent support coordinators keep loved ones close.

Research & Innovation

Actively participating in 14 clinical trials, our team advances the science of neonatal medicine while applying the latest evidence-based practices every day.

Transport & Outreach Network

Our 24/7 neonatal transport team safely transfers critically ill newborns from referring hospitals across the region to our specialized center.

What We Offer

Specialized NICU Services

From the most fragile preemies to complex surgical cases, our multidisciplinary team provides comprehensive neonatal care.

Cardiac Care

On-site pediatric cardiology and pediatric cardiac surgery for newborns with congenital heart defects, arrhythmias, and hemodynamic instability.

Cardiology Unit

Respiratory Support

Advanced ventilation including high-frequency oscillatory ventilation, inhaled nitric oxide therapy, and ECMO for severe respiratory failure.

Pulmonology

Neuroprotective Care

Continuous EEG monitoring, therapeutic hypothermia (cooling therapy), and comprehensive neurodevelopmental follow-up programs.

Neurology

Nutritional Support

Dedicated NICU dietitians, lactation consultants, and a donor human milk bank to optimize nutrition for the smallest patients.

Nutrition & Lactation

Neonatal Surgery

Pediatric surgeons specializing in gastrointestinal emergencies, abdominal wall defects, tracheoesophageal fistula, and other surgical conditions.

Surgery

Family Support Services

Social workers, chaplains, psychologists, and parent support groups to help families navigate the emotional journey of the NICU.

Psychosocial Support
